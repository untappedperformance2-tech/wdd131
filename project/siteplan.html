Mountain Bike Trails of Utah

Website Planning Document

Site Name

Mountain Bike Trails of Utah

I chose this name because it's straightforward and clearly tells visitors what the site is about. "Mountain Bike Trails of Utah" immediately communicates that the site focuses on mountain biking trails specifically in Utah. It's easy to remember and includes relevant keywords that people might search for. The name also has a professional feel while being welcoming to both beginner and experienced riders.

Optional domain availability: utahmtbtrails.org

Site Purpose

The site serves as a comprehensive resource for mountain bikers looking to explore trails across Utah. It provides:

  • Detailed trail listings with difficulty ratings, length, elevation gain, and location information
  • A submission form where users can recommend new trails to add to the database
  • A personal trail log where users can track which trails they've ridden using localStorage
  • Filtering and sorting options to help users find trails that match their skill level

The goal is to help riders discover new trails, share their experiences, and build their personal trail resume.

Scenarios

Scenario 1: "I'm visiting Utah for the first time and want to find beginner-friendly mountain bike trails near Salt Lake City. What trails would be good for someone who's new to mountain biking?"

Scenario 2: "I just rode an amazing trail that isn't listed on this site. How can I share it with other riders so they can enjoy it too?"

Scenario 3: "I want to keep track of all the trails I've completed this summer. Is there a way to save my progress and see which trails I still need to ride?"

Color Schema

#2C5E2E
#8B5A2B
#F5E6D3
#E67E22
  • Deep Forest Green (#2C5E2E) - Used for headers, navigation bar, and footer background. Represents the natural forest trails.
  • Earth Brown (#8B5A2B) - Used for borders, card accents, and secondary elements. Represents the dirt trails.
  • Sand Tan (#F5E6D3) - Used for page background and card backgrounds. Creates a natural, warm feel.
  • Burnt Orange (#E67E22) - Used for buttons, hover effects, and call-to-action elements. Adds energy and visual interest.

Typography

Montserrat (Google Fonts) - Used for headings (h1, h2, h3) and navigation links. Bold, clean, and modern - perfect for creating visual hierarchy.

Open Sans (Google Fonts) - Used for body text, paragraphs, and card descriptions. Highly readable and professional.
